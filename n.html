clacker works (ˈklak-ər ˌwərks) n.

1. A secretive or semi-secret development facility or project specializing in rapid prototyping and innovation in electromechanical engineering, often incorporating a retro-futuristic or steampunk aesthetic.
Example: “The clacker works was rumored to be developing intricate clockwork mechanisms integrated with cutting-edge vacuum tube electronics.”

2. A methodology of engineering and development characterized by:
a. Rapid, iterative design and construction of electromechanical systems.
b. Emphasis on intricate mechanical systems integrated with electronic components.
c. A raw, hands-on, and often noisy work environment, filled with the sounds of gears and electrical hums.
d. A fusion of vintage and futuristic technology, such as steam-powered automata with integrated circuitry.
Example: “Their clacker works approach to robotics prioritized robust mechanical designs augmented by analog electronic controls.”

etymology: Blend of clacker (referencing intricate, often noisy mechanical devices, particularly in the works of William Gibson) and Skunk Works (Lockheed Martin’s Advanced Development Programs, known for secretive and rapid innovation).

related terms: electromechanical, steampunk, retro-futurism, analog computing.
